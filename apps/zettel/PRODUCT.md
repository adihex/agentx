# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single thinker/knowledge-worker (the maker) capturing ideas as they happen — typed thoughts and spoken voice memos — and returning later to traverse the web of connections they've built. Context of use: mid-thought at a desk, or offloading an idea on the move. They want to get a thought *out of their head and into the system* with near-zero friction, and to trust that it will resurface and connect to prior thinking when it matters.

## Product Purpose

A personal Zettelkasten. Every thought becomes an atomic note (typed, or audio → transcribed via whisper.cpp); an AI partner then searches the existing notes and proposes/draws links to related ideas, so a connected body of knowledge compounds over time. Success looks like: the maker reaches for this instead of a scratchpad, capture feels effortless, and weeks later they rediscover a non-obvious link they'd have otherwise lost.

## Brand Personality

Calm, considered, quietly intelligent. A quiet study, not a chatty assistant. Three words: **calm, considered, text-forward**. Using it should feel like the interface steps out of the way so thinking is the foreground — a still room for ideas. Distinctive through restraint and typographic craft, never through visual volume.

## Anti-references

- **The current dark-glass SaaS dashboard** it ships with today: gradient title text, frosted-glass cards, indigo-on-near-black. This is the explicit thing to escape.
- **Cluttered, multi-panel dashboards** where badges and boxes compete and nothing breathes.
- **Sterile corporate admin panels** — dead gray, cold, soulless enterprise tooling.
- **Childish / bubbly / cartoonish** UI — too casual for serious thought.
- **Generic "AI slop"** — uniform icon-cards, all-caps eyebrow kickers above every section, hero-metric templates, gradient text, decorative glassmorphism.
- **CRITICAL — not the cream/sand/beige "warm editorial restraint" look** either. That warm-tinted near-white is the saturated 2026 AI default. Calm must be carried by type, whitespace, and a tight committed palette — not by a parchment background.

## Design Principles

1. **The text is the interface.** Notes and the thought being captured are the hero. Chrome, badges, and panels earn their place or disappear.
2. **Capture friction approaches zero.** The single most important act — getting a thought in — is always one motion away, never buried behind UI.
3. **Connection over collection.** The job isn't storing notes, it's making links visible and inviting. Surfacing "this relates to…" is the product, not a side feature.
4. **Calm by restraint, not decoration.** Quiet comes from whitespace, hierarchy, and a disciplined palette — never from glass, gradients, or ornament.
5. **Distinctive without loud.** It should feel like a considered, identifiable space — memorable through typographic detail and craft, not through shouting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, placeholders held to the same body contrast (no faint gray). Fully keyboard-operable — capture, search, and link traversal all reachable without a mouse. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored on every animation (crossfade/instant fallback). Reading measure capped at 65–75ch so note bodies stay legible.
